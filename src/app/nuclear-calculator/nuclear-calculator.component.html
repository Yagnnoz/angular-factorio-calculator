Nuclear Calculator

Calculating your nuclear needs is a bit more difficult than just plugging in the values. You need to actually do some math on your own here, but nothing to worry about, as this tool tells you how it works.

First Step - Reactor Arrangement

Draw a copy of you reactor arrangement. Each reactor gets a value of 1 and empty spaces get a 0. The example below uses a 3x3 grid with 8 reactors in total.
[1][1][1] [1][1][1] [1][0][1]

Second Step - Neighbor bonuses

Each reactor gets a production bonus for each reactor that they share a full side with. Back to the drawing - For each neighbor that shares a full side with a reactor add 1 to the number
[1+2][1+3][1+2] -> [3][4][3] [1+3][1+3][1+3] -> [4][4][4] [1+1][0+0][1+1] -> [2][0][2]

Third Step - Add the numbers

Now add the numbers you calculated together and enter the number in the input field.
